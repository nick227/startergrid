# Go-To-Market Playbook

## First Offer

Lead with:

```txt
Launch Audit + Storefront Readiness Setup
```

Do not lead with:

```txt
We post you everywhere automatically.
```

## Best Prospect

- independent used dealer
- 10 to 100 vehicles
- weak website or no clean inventory site
- inconsistent photos/listing data
- wants Google/Meta/marketplace exposure
- owner or GM still handles vendor follow-up

## Discovery Questions

- Where is your inventory listed today?
- Who updates price/photo/sold status?
- Do you have a clean inventory export?
- Which marketplaces have rejected or stalled you?
- Do your vehicle photos have public URLs?
- Who owns your website/domain?
- Do you have dealer license/W9/insurance docs ready?
- How many leads did your website generate last month?

## Sales Demo Flow

1. Show dealer profile completeness.
2. Show inventory readiness.
3. Show owned storefront listing.
4. Show platform status grid.
5. Open one feedable artifact.
6. Open one assisted marketplace packet.
7. Show blocker/next-action copy.
8. Show proof folder.

## Pricing Talk Track

Setup fee pays for cleanup and launch work.

Monthly fee pays for:

- hosted storefront
- readiness monitoring
- inventory sync checks
- lead routing
- platform status tracking

Assisted channel fee pays for manual partner workflow.

## Objection Handling

"My DMS already does this."

Response:

```txt
Great. We are not replacing it. We use your dealer/inventory data to prove which channels are launch-ready and track external activation work.
```

"Can you guarantee Cars.com/Autotrader approval?"

Response:

```txt
No. We can prepare the packet, validate the data, track the handoff, and show exactly what is pending. Partner approval stays with the partner.
```

"Why do I need a storefront?"

Response:

```txt
It gives every vehicle a clean landing page, creates immediate lead capture, and gives feed/ad channels a controlled destination.
```

## First 20 Customer Goal

Do not optimize for scale first.

Learn:

- common missing fields
- photo quality problems
- actual dealer willingness to pay
- channel demand order
- support hours per setup
- which partner packets create traction
