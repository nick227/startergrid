Listing content is subject to federal and state advertising rules, OEM program standards where applicable, and each marketplace’s posting policy. Dealers remain responsible for what is published under their name.

## Price and fees

Advertised price must be obtainable by a typical buyer under the conditions shown. Separate doc fees, accessories, or conditional rebates belong in the disclosure text required by your state — not buried off the listing.

Example: Internet price $19,995 with “plus $899 doc” only in fine print has drawn state AG attention in multiple jurisdictions.

## Availability and mileage

A funded retail deal should not remain **available** on syndicated channels. Odometer and title-brand status must match the physical unit. Recon in progress is not an excuse to list mileage or equipment the buyer will not receive.

## Photos and description

Images must depict the actual VIN offered. Using a stock photo for a base trim when the unit is loaded, or a prior year’s body style, is treated as misrepresentation on several major classifieds.

## Platform rules

Marketplaces add requirements beyond statute: mandatory dealer display name, VIN in description, limits on ALL CAPS headlines, watermarks, and prohibited claims (“guaranteed approval,” incorrect “new” labeling on titles).

A row can pass internal validation and still fail platform review.
